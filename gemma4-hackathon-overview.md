## Description

The Build with Gemma 4: ML, AI, Deep Learning & NLP Community Hackathon invites university students across Bangladesh to build innovative AI applications using Gemma 4, Google's latest family of open models.

Whether you're solving challenges in education, healthcare, accessibility, productivity, agriculture, finance, climate, or social impact, this hackathon is your opportunity to transform an idea into a working AI application.

This is a hybrid hackathon. Students from Southeast University are welcome to participate in person during the offline event, while students from any university in Bangladesh can build and submit their projects online through the Kaggle Community Hackathon platform.

Projects must use Gemma 4 as the primary AI model and demonstrate meaningful integration of its capabilities to solve a real-world problem.

## Your Goal

Build an AI application powered by Gemma 4 that addresses a real-world problem.

Your submission should clearly demonstrate:

- The problem you are solving
- Why it matters
- How Gemma 4 is integrated into your solution
- The technical implementation and architecture
- A working prototype or live demonstration
- The potential real-world impact of your application

Projects will be evaluated based on:

- 💡 Innovation (30%)
- ⚙️ Technical Implementation (30%)
- 🌍 Real-world Impact (20%)
- 🎨 User Experience (10%)
- 🎤 Presentation & Demo (10%)

A strong submission combines technical excellence with a compelling story, demonstrating both what you built and why it creates meaningful value.

## Submission Requirements

A valid submission must include all of the following:

- Kaggle Writeup
- Public Code Repository (GitHub or Kaggle Notebook)
- Working Demo (Hosted application, video demonstration, or runnable Kaggle Notebook)

All submissions must be completed and submitted through the Kaggle Community Hackathon platform before the submission deadline. Draft or unsubmitted Writeups will not be considered for judging.

To create a submission, click the New Writeup button on the competition page. After saving your Writeup, click Submit in the top-right corner to officially enter the competition. Each team may submit one Writeup, which can be edited, unsubmitted, and resubmitted any number of times before the submission deadline.

Note: If you attach a private Kaggle Resource to your public Kaggle Writeup, it will automatically become public after the submission deadline.

## Additional Requirements

- Projects must use Gemma 4 as the primary AI model.
- Gemma 4 must be the only Large Language Model (LLM) used to power the application's AI capabilities.
- Teams may use traditional machine learning libraries, computer vision models, OCR libraries, speech processing libraries, databases, vector databases, APIs, and other non-generative AI techniques where appropriate, provided they support the application and do not replace Gemma 4 as the primary AI model.
- The use of any other LLMs or generative foundation models is not permitted.
- Projects that do not demonstrate meaningful integration of Gemma 4 may be disqualified from judging.

## Evaluation

The judging process is designed to be swift and impactful, reflecting the rapid 1-day sprint format of this event. Our panel of experts will evaluate your submissions based on how effectively you utilize Gemma 4 models to build innovative and practical applications.

Rather than expecting production-ready polish, judges will focus on the core functionality, creativity, and real-world impact of your prototypes. Below is the official scoring rubric that will be used to evaluate each Kaggle Writeup and accompanying demo:

| Criteria | Description | Points |
|---|---|---|
| Gemma Integration (30%) | Did they effectively utilize Gemma 4? Is the model core to the solution? | 0-30 |
| Innovation & Impact (30%) | Does the app solve a meaningful problem? Is the approach creative and relevant? | 0-30 |
| Functionality (20%) | Does the prototype actually work? Is the demo convincing? | 0-20 |
| Presentation & Writeup (20%) | Is the Kaggle Writeup clear? Does it clearly explain the problem and solution? | 0-20 |
